Start a ministry

”Therefore go and make disciples of all nations, baptising them in the name of the Father and of the Son and of the Holy Spirit, and teaching them to obey everything I have commanded you.” (Matthew 28:19, 20)

You want to start a ministry within Newlife?

We can guide you to start a ministry of your own and grow it successfully.

Do you feel that God is calling you to start a ministry that makes disciples and serves to advance His Kingdom and prepare men and women for the End Time? This page is designed to help anyone with a calling to start a ministry that glorifies God. Do not be afraid: one of the exciting moments in the church is when you see a member of the congregation move from spectator to participant. Where they once only attended on the weekend and occasional seasonal events, they now feel they have found their purpose: God has given them a vision to start a new ministry.

Starting your own ministry means you’re fulfilling the Great Commission and there is nothing questionable provided that everything is done within the context of Adventist values.

Branches that make up the great vine...

Given the guided understanding by Christ that we are branches that make up the great vine (John 15:5 I am the vine, you are the branches),and also inspired by Christ's great commission to "Go and make disciples", the modern-day Seventh-day Adventists need to recognize that they are in the last days of this earth’s history and need to organize themselves, so they could more effectively and more widely proclaim the third angel's message. Founded in love for our Savior and His love for sinners, we need to proclaim Christ and Him crucified, His longing that men and women be whole, and His desire that we "keep the commandments of God and the faith of Jesus" (Rev. 14:12).

The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few...

Matthew 9:37 says "The harvest is plentiful but the workers are few.” In other words there is so much work to be done. The Holy Spirit has prepared the hearts of souls and they are ready for the Word. They are ready to be impacted. There are millions of souls who need to be brought into the light of the Remnant Church for truth in the Spirit of Prophesy and these last days and church members who have a calling need not be spectators, they need to move in and take part to reform not only theology, but also lifestyle, promoting radical health reforms and prioritizing the Three Angels Message in the Spirit of Prophesy. They need to preach prophetic truths, and there are so many ways of doing that in this new information tech-savvy age.

Start-up proposal...

Essentially, you as the the start-up leader needs to think through and prepare a “proposal” for discussion. Addressing the following items (at the least there may be more) will bring clarification to the possible new ministry start-up: 
•           Purpose. What is the purpose of the ministry?
•           Values. What are the values?
•           Needs Assessment. Why do you think this ministry is needed?
•           Alignment. How does this new ministry align with the vision of the Seventh-day Adventist Church?
•           Short-Term and Long-Term Goals. What are the intended results for the first year? What is your ultimate dream for this ministry?
•           Resources. What will the budgetary needs be for start-up costs and yearly expenses? How many volunteers will be needed and how do you plan to recruit them? Is staff time required and if so, who will be the champion?

Below are guidelines...

1. Prayerfully study the Great Commission and the inspired counsels on reaching out to “the stranger in our midst.”

2. Share your inspired calling with the  Church Board and Pastor and talk to  a Prayer Ministry Coordinator who is to:
•           Pastoral affirmation. Ask God to affirm this ministry and bless it.
•           Prayer Ministry engagement. Start praying with this ministry.
•           Alignment. Envision the ministry recognizing God’s leading (1 Cor 12:9).
•           Personal Ministry consultation. Create practical engagement with our church's Personal Ministry department for useful collaboration and get the most value out their insights and support.

Pastoral Affirmation

Pastoral is critical. The pastor is your shepherd and mentor from the pulpit. The pastor must be part of inspiring this idea and move you to action. Pstoral affirmation is a great blessing. In fact the pastor will guide you through the whole process because this is one of his key mandates: to get church members to start their own miniseries. So the pastor will share in your excitement.

 

If you are our member who wants to start your own ministry within the Newlife Seventh-day Adventist Church, please contact the church office during regular business hours at +254 (795) 638070 or by sending us an email. THANKS for your help and be blessed abundantly!

About Newlife

Newlife Seventh-day Adventist Church is a diverse Christian community comprised of individuals that seek to love God, each other and the greater community of Nairobi. Our church is a part of a worldwide community of Seventh-day Adventists. We share a love for Jesus and a commitment to demonstrating and spreading the good news of His transforming love to our neighborhoods, the city of Nairobi, and the world..

Our Mission

The Mission of the Seventh-day Adventist Church is to Proclaim to all the people the Everlasting Gospel in the context of the Three Angels Message of Revelation 14 : 6-12 leading them to accept Jesus as personal Saviour and to unite with His church, and nurturing them in preparation for His soon return.
